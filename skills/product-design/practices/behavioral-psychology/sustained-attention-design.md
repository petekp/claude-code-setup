## Principle
Human sustained attention degrades predictably after 10-20 minutes of continuous engagement — design natural pause points, transitions, and recovery moments into long workflows to maintain cognitive performance.

## Why It Matters
Attention research consistently shows that vigilance and sustained focus decline after approximately 10-15 minutes of continuous task engagement. This is not a failure of willpower; it is a neurological reality — the brain's attentional resources deplete and require micro-recovery periods to reset. Products that demand unbroken attention for 30-60 minutes (long forms, extended reading, complex configuration wizards) will see increasing error rates and decreasing quality of user decisions as the session progresses. Designing intentional breaks respects this biological constraint.

## Application Guidelines
- Break workflows longer than 10 minutes into distinct phases with natural stopping points — give users permission to pause and return
- Insert transitional moments between phases: a progress summary, a brief animation, or a "Great work so far" acknowledgment that provides a micro-break
- Auto-save state continuously so users can step away at any pause point without losing progress
- For content consumption (articles, courses, documentation), insert visual breaks (images, whitespace, section dividers) every 3-5 minutes of reading time
- Avoid creating uninterruptible flows where the user feels trapped — every step should feel like it can be the stopping point

## Anti-Patterns
- Designing a 45-minute onboarding wizard with no save points, no progress indication, and no way to pause and resume — users who are interrupted (or whose attention fades) must start over, guaranteeing abandonment
