## Principle
Organize related pieces of information into meaningful groups (chunks) so users can process them as single conceptual units rather than individual elements.

## Why It Matters
Working memory handles chunks, not raw data points. George Miller's research established that working memory holds approximately 7 (plus or minus 2) items, but subsequent research refined this to 3-5 chunks for complex information. A phone number formatted as 415-555-1234 is three chunks; presented as 4155551234, it's ten individual digits competing for working memory slots. The same principle applies to every list, menu, dashboard, and content layout in software. Well-chunked information dramatically increases the amount users can process at a glance, reduces scanning time, and improves recall.

## Application Guidelines
- Group related form fields under labeled sections of 3-5 related fields (e.g., "Shipping Address," "Payment Information") rather than presenting 15 fields in a single undivided form
- Format data for scannability: use separators in numbers, group list items under category headers, and break text into short paragraphs
- Limit each chunk to 3-5 items; larger groups should be subdivided with visual separators or subheadings
- Use cards, bordered sections, or subtle background colors to visually reinforce grouping
- In navigation, group related menu items under category labels rather than presenting a flat list
- Format data in meaningful groups: phone numbers as (555) 123-4567, credit cards as 4-digit blocks, dates with clear separators
- When presenting options, group them into named categories of 3-5 items rather than a flat list of 20

## Anti-Patterns
- Long undifferentiated lists with no grouping, headers, or visual separators
- Dense data tables with no row grouping or section breaks
- Presenting phone numbers, credit card numbers, or ID strings as unformatted continuous strings
- Flat navigation menus with 20+ items and no categorical organization
- Displaying a settings page as a single scrolling list of 40+ ungrouped toggles with no section headers
