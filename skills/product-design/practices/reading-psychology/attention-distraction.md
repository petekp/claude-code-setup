## Principle
When users need to read, comprehend, or make decisions, suppress all non-essential visual and interactive stimuli that compete for attention.

## Why It Matters
Attention is a zero-sum resource. Every animation, notification badge, auto-playing element, or peripheral call-to-action steals cognitive bandwidth from the primary reading task. Research in inattentional blindness shows that even subtle peripheral motion can disrupt comprehension and increase error rates. The cost is not just distraction — it is the cognitive effort required to refocus after each interruption. Studies estimate that it takes 15-25 seconds to fully re-engage with a reading task after an attentional interruption, even a brief one.

## Application Guidelines
- Suppress non-critical notifications, banners, and animations while users are in focused reading or form-filling contexts
- Avoid auto-playing carousels, videos, or animations adjacent to content that requires comprehension
- Dim or reduce visual prominence of secondary navigation and sidebar content during focused tasks (reading, checkout, onboarding)
- Use modal or focused overlays for tasks requiring concentration, removing the background UI from competition
- Queue non-urgent notifications and surface them at natural task boundaries (page transitions, task completion) rather than mid-task

## Anti-Patterns
- Displaying a promotional banner, a chat widget, a cookie consent popup, and a newsletter modal simultaneously while the user is trying to read an article — each element fragments attention and collectively makes focused reading nearly impossible
