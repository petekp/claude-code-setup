## Principle
Bold the most information-carrying words and phrases within body text so that scanning users can extract meaning without reading every word.

## Why It Matters
Users scan before they read — they look for keywords that signal relevance to their current goal. When key phrases are bolded, they pop out of the surrounding text and create a "scanning layer" that communicates the gist of each paragraph in seconds. Research by the Nielsen Norman Group found that highlighted keywords within running text significantly improved scanning success and reduced time-to-find for specific information. Bolding acts as a secondary navigation system within body content.

## Application Guidelines
- Bold 2-3 key phrases per paragraph maximum; over-bolding creates noise and defeats the purpose
- Bold the concept or term, not filler words — emphasize the noun or noun phrase that carries the meaning
- Use bold exclusively for emphasis; avoid using italic, underline, or ALL CAPS for emphasis in UI text (underline implies links; italic reduces legibility; caps feels aggressive)
- Ensure bolded phrases form a coherent summary when read in isolation — a user skimming only the bold text should understand the key points
- Do not bold entire sentences or paragraphs; this eliminates the contrast that makes emphasis work

## Anti-Patterns
- Bolding entire paragraphs or most of the text on a page, which destroys the visual contrast that makes emphasis useful and results in a page where nothing is effectively emphasized
