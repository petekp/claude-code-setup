## Principle
Generous whitespace between and around content elements reduces cognitive load, improves comprehension, and increases the perceived quality of the interface.

## Why It Matters
Whitespace is not wasted space — it is active design. Research from the Wichita State University readability studies found that increased margins and padding around text improved reading comprehension by up to 20%. Whitespace performs three cognitive functions: it groups related items (proximity principle), it separates unrelated items (creating visual boundaries without lines), and it gives the eye resting points that prevent fatigue during extended reading. Cramped interfaces feel stressful even before users read a single word.

## Application Guidelines
- Use at least 1.5x the font size as paragraph spacing, and at least 2x as section spacing to create clear content boundaries
- Maintain consistent padding within components (cards, modals, form groups) — internal whitespace should follow a spacing scale (4px, 8px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px)
- Increase whitespace around high-priority elements (CTAs, key metrics, alerts) to isolate them and draw attention
- Resist the urge to fill empty screen space with decorative elements or low-value content; deliberate emptiness signals confidence
- Use whitespace asymmetry intentionally — tighter spacing signals grouping, wider spacing signals separation

## Anti-Patterns
- Packing every pixel with content, controls, and decoration to maximize "information density" — this creates visual claustrophobia that degrades both readability and perceived quality, ultimately causing users to absorb less information, not more
