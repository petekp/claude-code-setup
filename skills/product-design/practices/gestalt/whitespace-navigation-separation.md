## Principle
Generous whitespace between navigation groups leverages the proximity principle to create clear visual separation without relying on borders or dividers.

## Why It Matters
Navigation is the structural skeleton of an application. When nav items are evenly spaced with no grouping, users must read every label to find what they need. Strategic whitespace creates perceptual clusters that match the information architecture, letting users jump to the right group before scanning individual items. This accelerates wayfinding and reduces cognitive overhead.

## Application Guidelines
- Use noticeably larger gaps between navigation groups than between items within a group (a ratio of at least 2:1)
- In sidebar navigation, separate primary sections with whitespace rather than horizontal rules to keep the visual weight low
- Align whitespace-based groups with the underlying information architecture so visual clusters match mental categories
- Combine whitespace with subtle section labels for complex nav structures where spacing alone is insufficient

## Anti-Patterns
- Using uniform spacing for all navigation items regardless of their hierarchical grouping
- Inserting heavy divider lines between every nav section when whitespace alone would create the grouping
- Compressing navigation into a tight list with minimal spacing, destroying all proximity-based grouping signals
