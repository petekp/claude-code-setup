## Principle
A call-to-action placed far from its related content breaks the proximity principle, causing users to miss the action or associate it with the wrong context.

## Why It Matters
Proximity is one of the strongest grouping forces in perception. When a CTA drifts away from the content it relates to -- pushed to the bottom of a long page, separated by unrelated modules, or isolated in a sidebar -- users either do not find it or misattribute it to nearby but unrelated content. Conversion rates and task completion suffer directly.

## Application Guidelines
- Place the primary CTA immediately adjacent to the content or decision it supports, within the same visual group
- On long-form pages, repeat the CTA near relevant decision points rather than relying on a single instance far from context
- In card-based layouts, anchor the action button inside the card boundary so proximity binds it to the card content
- Avoid inserting unrelated banners, ads, or secondary content between a descriptive block and its associated action

## Anti-Patterns
- Placing a "Subscribe" button at the page footer when the subscription pitch is in the hero section
- Separating a product description from its "Add to Cart" button with reviews, cross-sells, or other unrelated modules
- Putting form submission buttons outside the visible form container or far below the last input field
