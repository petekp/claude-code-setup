## Principle
Hero sections must establish an unambiguous figure-ground relationship so the primary message and call-to-action are instantly perceived as the foreground against the background imagery or color.

## Why It Matters
Landing pages live or die on first impression. If the hero image competes with the headline for visual dominance, neither registers as the clear figure. Users hesitate, skip the content, or bounce. A strong figure-ground separation ensures the value proposition lands within the first second of attention.

## Application Guidelines
- Use semi-transparent overlays or gradient scrims on background images to push them perceptually into the ground layer
- Ensure headline text has sufficient contrast against every region of the background image it overlaps, not just the average
- Size the primary CTA large enough and give it enough contrast to read as a distinct foreground object against both the image and the text
- Test with real imagery (not placeholder stock photos) since busy photographs can undermine figure-ground clarity that worked with simple backgrounds

## Anti-Patterns
- Placing white text over a bright, high-detail photograph without any overlay or text shadow
- Using a background image so visually compelling that it dominates the hero and the headline becomes the ground
- Allowing parallax or animation effects that continuously shift the figure-ground relationship, disorienting users
